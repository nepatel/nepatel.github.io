"With great power comes great responsibility."

I love tech.
I love people.
However, the intersection of tech AND people? Yikes.
Two thoughts.

  1. The government (read: legislators) and the general public's understanding of technology, its capabilities, its limitations, and, most importantly, its dangers are flawed.
  2. This tech illiteracy opens a nasty door. One that leads to the abuse of technology in ways that can undermine the freedom, equality, and safety of people.

I encourage my fellow developers and potential employers to critically reflect on how their work impacts real human lives.
Examples include biases in algorithms, exposing sensitive data, infringing on privacy, capitilizing on childrens' naiviety, broadcasting messages of hatred, disseminating misinformation and "fake news", etc.
These issues are beyond obviously malicious work, such as scamming, phishing, and black-hat hacking.
We are not developing in a bubble.
We are exposing the framework of reality and altering the source code of lives through 1s and 0s.
TLDR: We have an ethical responsibility to think about and act upon the societal consequences of our work as developers, designers, engineers, and computer scientists.
